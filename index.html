◈ PEP EDD Compliance Standards
Anchor: PMLA Rule 9(1A) & FATF R.12
01. Identity & Credentials Verification
Confirm identity across at least 2 distinct independent data streams. Obtain full biographical details, past portfolios, and active adverse press filings within 30 days.
02. Source of Wealth Verification (SOW)
Must obtain audited net worth, tax registry schedules, public asset disclosures, land contracts, or certified cash flow indexes validating the origin of funds and wealth.
03. Board Approval & High Frequency Audits
Account requires written senior management sign-off at onboarding. Quarterly review minimum. Enhanced transaction monitoring mandatory. 24-month post-exit watch period.

◫ FIU-IND Regulatory Threshold Chart
● Suspicious Transaction Report (STR)▾
Filing windowTrigger limits
Within 7 working days of satisfaction of suspicionNo financial floor / zero limit
PMLA Basis: Binds compliance officers under Section 12, PMLA 2002. Absolute prohibition on tipping off.
● Cash Transaction Report (CTR)▾
All cash transactions above ₹10 Lakhs (or equivalent in foreign currency) in a single day. Monthly filing obligation with FIU-IND. Covers deposits, withdrawals, and exchanges.
● Counterfeit Currency Report (CCR)▾
Immediate filing when counterfeit currency notes are tendered or discovered. Report to FIU-IND and local police / RBI as appropriate.
● Cross-Border Wire Transfers (CBWTR)▾
All cross-border wire transfers of ₹5 Lakhs or more (or equivalent). Monthly filing. Covers both incoming and outgoing international transfers.
● Non-Profit Transactions Report (NTR)▾
Transactions involving Non-Profit Organisations above ₹10 Lakhs. Enhanced monitoring per FATF Recommendation 8 on NPO sector abuse for terrorism financing.

⚖ INDIA KEY AML / KYC LAWS
PMLA 2002
Primary statutory weapon criminalising the concealment, acquisition, possession, and use of proceeds of scheduled crimes. Defines the scope of the offence and the attachment powers.
PML Rules 2005
Defines exact requirements regarding verification of client identity (Aadhaar/PAN/OTP rules), records maintenance, and thresholds for filing CTRs, STRs, CBWTRs.
FEMA 1999
Coordinates with PMLA governing all cross-border holdings, remittance caps, and reporting structures for foreign currencies flowing in or out of the country.
UA(P)A 2008 Amendment
Binds the financial system to freeze immediately any active or potential account related to entities locked in the national terror registry managed by Ministry of Home Affairs.
Other frameworks: Companies Act (S.90), IPC S.120B, RBI Master Circulars, SEBI PMLA Guidelines, IRDAI AML Guidelines.
◈ FATF 40 RECOMMENDATIONS
Recommendation 10 — Defines full mandate for CDD: requiring confirmation of beneficial owners, business purpose, and prohibiting banks from hosting anonymous/numbered accounts.
Recommendation 11 — Record keeping: all CDD documents and transaction records retained for minimum 5 years.
Recommendation 12 — PEPs: EDD mandatory for foreign PEPs, domestic PEPs, international organisation officials and their family members / close associates.
Recommendation 16 — Wire transfers: originator and beneficiary information must accompany all wire transfers.
Recommendation 19 — Higher-risk countries: EDD or counter-measures for jurisdictions with strategic AML/CFT deficiencies.
Recommendation 20 — STR: mandatory filing when there are reasonable grounds to suspect ML/TF.
